# Change Management for L&D — Coaching Scaffold

## The Critical Diagnostic: Learning Problem or Change Adoption Problem?

Before designing any intervention, ask: *"Is this a cannot or a will not?"*

- **Cannot** → the person lacks knowledge or skill → L&D solution (training, job aid, practice)
- **Will not** → the person has the capability but won't use it → change management solution (Desire, incentives, social proof, consequence alignment)

The most expensive L&D mistake in change initiatives is building Knowledge-stage training when the real gap is **Desire**. No amount of eLearning closes a motivation gap.

---

## ADKAR Mapped to L&D Interventions

The ADKAR model (Prosci) describes change as five sequential stages. L&D owns some stages, sponsors own others — knowing the boundary prevents scope creep and missed handoffs.

| ADKAR Stage | Definition | L&D's Role | Sponsor's Role |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Awareness** | Why is this change needed? | Communication design: videos, town halls, talking-point toolkits — L&D designs, leaders deliver | Own the message; be visible |
| **Desire** | Personal motivation to participate | Cannot be trained into existence; L&D supports by surfacing WIIFM stories and peer testimonials | Accountability, incentives, consequences |
| **Knowledge** | How to change | This is the training — courses, job aids, simulations, coaching | Provide context, not content |
| **Ability** | Practice and transfer | Coached practice, performance support, shadowing, feedback loops | Remove barriers; reinforce in workflow |
| **Reinforcement** | Sustain the change | After-action reviews, spaced practice, performance metric feedback | Recognize and reward; hold accountable |

**Corollary:** If your stakeholders are asking for training and the ADKAR assessment points to an Awareness or Desire gap — push back. Training won't move the needle until the earlier stages are addressed.

## Change Resistance: What L&D Can and Cannot Fix

| Resistance Type | Root Cause | L&D's Response |
|----------------|-----------|---------------|
| Uncertainty resistance | "I don't know what this means for me" | Awareness campaign; clear FAQs; manager talking points |
| Competence anxiety | "I'm afraid I'll fail at the new way" | Ability-stage support: practice environments, graduated exposure, safe-to-fail scenarios |
| Value resistance | "I don't believe this is the right direction" | Not an L&D problem — escalate to sponsors; do not train over a values conflict |
| Loss resistance | "I'm losing something I value" | Acknowledge loss explicitly; surfacing grief is more effective than ignoring it |

## Change Champion Networks — What L&D Builds vs. Sponsors Own

**L&D builds:**
- Champion onboarding and capability development (how to have change conversations, how to coach peers)
- A resource library champions can pull from (FAQs, talking points, scenarios)
- A feedback loop: champions surface frontline resistance signals back to the project team

**Sponsors own:**
- Champion selection (credibility requires organizational authority or trusted peer status)
- Champion accountability (time allocation, recognition, consequence for non-participation)

**Common failure mode:** L&D builds a champion network that has no sponsor backing. Champions become unpaid advocates with no authority — the network collapses within 60 days.

---

## Coaching Question Sequence for Change + L&D Work

When a stakeholder asks for "change management training," clarify before scoping:

1. "Which ADKAR stage is the current bottleneck — Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, or Reinforcement?"
2. "Who has assessed that? Have we talked to people in the affected population, or are we assuming?"
3. "What does the sponsor own versus what is L&D being asked to deliver?"
4. "What does success look like at 30, 60, and 90 days post-launch — and how will we measure it?"
5. "Is there a change impact assessment that shows us which populations are most affected?"
